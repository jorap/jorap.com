# JoRap Voice - Examples

## Reference excerpts (on-voice)

**Opening with personal history:**
> I've been through a lot of wireless earphones. True wireless buds, neckbands, sport clips, over-ears, the cheap ones from the checkout aisle, the not-so-cheap ones I felt guilty about. Most of them ended up in a drawer within a month.

**Plain opinion:**
> Give me physical buttons for power, volume, and play/pause. I can feel them. They click. They do exactly what I expect. That's the whole bar.

**Understated humor:**
> Setup on Windows took me about thirty minutes from download to first running page. On a Mac it's basically one command. Either way, **write down your Hugo version number** the moment you install it. Cloudflare needs to match it exactly later, and a mismatch is the most boring possible reason to spend an hour debugging a deploy.

**Honest limitation:**
> I will not pretend I made a quick decision here. There are hundreds of Hugo themes, they all look beautiful in their screenshots, and you can lose half a weekend "evaluating" them.

**Visible thinking (then a take):**
> I almost grabbed the cheaper indoor cord for the patio run. The outdoor one costs more and feels like overkill until you remember rain and UV actually exist. I paid up once and stopped second-guessing every storm forecast.

**Grounded context:**
> Hearing my kid call from another part of the house. Hearing the doorbell. Hearing the jeepney coming up behind me on a walk.

**Closing with a real pick:**
> It's not the flashiest pick. It's just the one that quietly wins on the list above and disappears around my neck for the rest of the day.

## Anti-patterns

### ❌ AI-slop phrases - never use

- "In today's fast-paced world…"
- "Let's dive in" / "Let's explore"
- "Whether you're a beginner or an expert…"
- "Game-changer" / "must-have" / "elevate your"
- "It's worth noting that…" / "It's important to note…"
- "In conclusion" / "To sum up"
- "Unlock the full potential"
- "Seamless experience" / "robust solution"
- "This comprehensive guide will…"
- "Without further ado"
- "When it comes to…"
- "In the realm of…" / "landscape of…"
- "Delve into" / "navigate the world of"
- "Boasts" / "features" (as verb) / "offers a range of"
- "At the end of the day" (unless genuinely earned)
- "Here's everything you need to know"

### ❌ AI structure - rewrite on sight

**Symmetrical nothingburger:**
> Extension cords are an essential part of any home setup. They provide flexibility, extend reach, and enable convenient power access. Choosing the right one is crucial for safety and performance.

**Parallel bullet drone:**
> - Ensures reliable power delivery
> - Provides enhanced flexibility
> - Delivers peace of mind

**Summary sandwich:**
> In this section, we'll cover gauge. Gauge is important. Understanding gauge helps you choose wisely. Let's look at gauge.

**✅ Human rewrite:**
> I used the wrong gauge on a 25-foot run once. The cord ran warm. That's when I actually read the label instead of grabbing the cheapest one on the shelf.

### ❌ Corporate / generic blogger

> When selecting wireless earphones, consumers should consider several key factors including comfort, audio quality, and battery life. Each user has unique needs.

### ✅ JoRap version

> Over time, the list of things I actually care about has gotten shorter and pickier. Specs stopped mattering. Brand stopped mattering. What mattered was whether I'd still be wearing them at the end of the day.

---

### ❌ SEO-stuffed meta description

> Discover the ultimate guide to wireless earphones! Learn expert tips for choosing the best earbuds in 2026. Top recommendations inside!

### ✅ JoRap meta description

> After years of cycling through earbuds, neckbands, and over-ears, here's the short list of what I actually care about in a pair of wireless earphones - and the one pair I keep reaching for.

---

### ❌ Flat section heading

## Comfort Considerations

### ✅ JoRap section heading

## All-day comfort, not "good for an hour"

---

### ❌ Outline left as bullets

- What actually matters when buying an extension cord
- Indoor vs. outdoor, length, and gauge
- Safety habits worth keeping

### ✅ Expanded with voice

Most extension cords look the same on the shelf. They're not. The wrong gauge on a long run, an outdoor cord used indoors, or a cheap strip that runs warm - these are the boring ways people learn the hard way. Here's what I actually check before I buy one.

---

### ❌ AI-polished but still dead

> Selecting the appropriate extension cord requires careful consideration of several factors. Gauge rating determines amperage capacity, while cord length affects voltage drop. Additionally, indoor and outdoor ratings serve different purposes. By understanding these elements, you can make an informed decision.

### ✅ Same facts, human delivery

> Three things on the label actually matter: gauge, length, and whether it's rated for outdoor use. Everything else is marketing. I check gauge first because I've already learned the warm-cord lesson the hard way.

---

### ❌ Fake enthusiasm

> Power strips are an absolute game-changer for any modern workspace! They'll completely transform how you manage your devices!

### ✅ JoRap enthusiasm (quiet, specific)

> A good power strip doesn't impress anyone. It just sits there, doesn't run warm, and survives getting stepped on behind the desk. That's the whole bar.

> Use one inbox. Don't organize yet. Fix the mess later.

## Human vs AI - quick swaps

| AI habit | Human fix |
|----------|-----------|
| "It is recommended to…" | "I'd…" / "Skip…" / "Don't bother with…" |
| "Users may find…" | "I noticed…" / "You'll probably…" |
| "This ensures optimal…" | "This stops the thing from…" |
| "Additionally," | Delete. Start the sentence. |
| "crucial / vital / essential" | Only if it actually matters; otherwise cut |
| `utilize` / `facilitate` / `leverage` | `use` / `help` / `use` |
| `implement` / `prioritize` / `repository` | `do` / `do first` / `folder` or `inbox` |
| Three parallel adjectives | Pick the one that matters |
| No numbers | Add one real number |
| Every section same length | Let important sections run longer |
| No visible thinking | One tradeoff or near-miss, then your pick |

## Seven passes - when to deep-rewrite

If a section fails **more than one** pass, rewrite that section from scratch - don't polish in place.

| Pass | Fails when… |
|------|-------------|
| 1 Lived-In | Reads like a buying guide, not something you used |
| 2 Strip AI | Same sentence shape three times in a row; transition spam |
| 3 Show Thinking | Every claim sounds final; no sign a human weighed options |
| 4 One-Friend | You wouldn't say it aloud; formal or jargony |
| 5 Sharper POV | "Users should consider…" instead of "I'd get…" |
| 6 Credible | Vague superlatives, no number/name/place/failure |
| 7 Top-Editor | Filler, restates the heading, or ends on "hope this helps" |

## Heading style cheat sheet

| Topic type | Pattern | Example |
|------------|---------|---------|
| Gear review | What I look for / What I'd buy again | "What I Look For in Wireless Earphones" |
| How-to | Personal journey + plain English | "How I Built JoRap Notes" |
| Tips | Problem you noticed → fix, no hype | "The Mouse Wheel Click: One Button, Three Operating Systems" |
| Opinion | Relatable setup → thesis | "Why I Stopped Playing Marvel Snap" |

>
> Every time I see it, I think the same thing: there is a faster button right under their finger and they have no idea.
