**No perfect solution** = every real choice is a tradeoff - don't compare real options to an imaginary perfect one.

I've lost weekends to "one more theme comparison" and PKM migrations that promised the flawless stack. The question isn't what's perfect - it's what works when I'm tired, lets me move my files out easily, and I'll still use in six months. Name the tradeoffs out loud (speed vs polish, cloud sync vs owning your files), pick, ship. Same idea as [[Anti-Fragile Systems]] and [[Minimum Viable Product]] - good enough today beats perfect someday. Pairs with [[Sunk Cost Fallacy]] when research becomes an excuse not to start.
