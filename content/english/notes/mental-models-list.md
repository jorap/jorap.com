**Mental models** = thinking shortcuts that survived real decisions - not every model on Wikipedia.

This page is a [[Maps of Content]]: useful lenses below, plus my own notes when a model keeps showing up in real choices. I don't collect the whole internet - I note what changed behavior.

## In this garden

Notes I've written because the model earned repeat use:

- [[First Principles Thinking]] - rebuild the claim from scratch
- [[Minimum Effective Dose]] - smallest effort that still works
- [[Minimum Viable Product]] - smallest thing worth shipping
- [[Sunk Cost Fallacy]] - past effort isn't a reason to continue
- [[Pareto Principle]] - find the slice that drives most results
- [[Low Hanging Fruit]] - easy wins worth picking first
- [[Compounding]] - small consistent gains stack over time
- [[Process Over Outcomes]] - focus on the habit you can rerun
- [[The Feynman Technique]] - teach it to find gaps
- [[There Is No Perfect Solution]] - good enough beats perfect someday
- [[Anti-Fragile Systems]] - systems that survive stress

## Performance & execution

Models that help you do more with less - the ones I reach for when I'm stuck or overbuilding:

- [[Pareto Principle]] - most results come from a small slice of work; find it, protect it
- [[Low Hanging Fruit]] - easy wins you can grab now; pick them before the hard climb
- **Parkinson's Law** - work expands to fill the time you give it; shorter deadlines, smaller scopes
- [[Compounding]] - small consistent gains stack; steady beats heroic weeks
- **Leverage** - one hour that saves you many hours later (templates, automation, reusable notes)
- **Theory of constraints** - find the slowest step; fixing that fixes the rest
- **Deliberate practice** - practice weak spots on purpose, not mindless repetition
- **OODA loop** - watch, decide, act, repeat - move faster when feedback is slow
- **Eisenhower matrix** - urgent vs important; most "urgent" mail isn't important

Pairs with [[Slow Productivity]], [[Creative Output]], and [[Spaced Repetition]] when the goal is finished work, not smarter procrastination.

## Decision-making

- **Inversion** - ask what guarantees failure, then avoid that
- **Second-order effects** - and then what? The first answer is rarely the last consequence
- **Opportunity cost** - every yes is a no to something else
- **Expected value** - what you gain on average, not the best-case story
- **Circle of competence** - know where your judgment is earned vs borrowed
- **Confirmation bias** - you'll find evidence for what you already believe; build in dissent
- **Hanlon's Razor** - don't assume malice when stupidity or friction explains it
- **Regression to the mean** - one great or terrible week often evens out; don't read too much into it

## Thinking & learning

- **Occam's Razor** - simpler explanation wins until evidence forces complexity
- **Map vs territory** - your notes are a map, not the real world
- **Availability bias** - recent or vivid stories feel more common than they are
- **Narrative fallacy** - your brain invents a clean story after messy events

Useful when reading news, [[RSS for Research]], or turning captures into [[Evergreen Notes]].

## Systems & risk

- **Margin of safety** - plan for wrong estimates and bad luck
- **Feedback loops** - some cycles grow, some snap back to normal; know which you're in
- **Redundancy** - one backup beats perfect setup with a single break point
- **Skin in the game** - weight advice by who pays when the call is wrong

Overlap with [[Future-Proofing Knowledge]] and [[Local-first Software]] when the system is your notes stack.
