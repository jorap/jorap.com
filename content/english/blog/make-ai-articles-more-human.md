AI-generated articles often fail the vibe check before the fact check. Not because the model is useless — because the output is **too smooth, too even, too same-length every sentence.**

Humans don't write like that. We burst. We shorten. We take a weird detour and come back.

---

## Perplexity and burstiness (plain English)

**Perplexity** — how varied and surprising the word choices feel. Higher isn't always better, but flat mid perplexity reads robotic.

**Burstiness** — variation in sentence length and structure. Humans mix short punches with longer winding sentences. Models default to uniform middle-length paragraphs.

When I want a draft that survives a human read, I ask for **both** — explicitly.

---

## The prompt I start with

From my notes — adapt the topic each time:

```
When it comes to writing content, two factors are crucial: "perplexity" and "burstiness." Perplexity measures the complexity of text. Separately, burstiness compares the variations of sentences. Humans tend to write with greater burstiness — some longer or complex sentences alongside shorter ones. AI sentences tend to be more uniform.

When writing the following content, I need a good amount of perplexity and burstiness. Do you understand?
```

Then I paste the brief, outline, or rough notes — not "write me an article about X" with no context.

---

## What still needs a human pass

AI can mimic shape. It can't replace **your**:

- Specific stories ("last Tuesday my router died…")
- Opinions you'll defend
- Local context (places, prices, culture)
- Judgment about what's true *today*

My workflow: **AI draft → human edit for specifics → cut 20% → read aloud**

If it sounds like a brochure when spoken, it goes back for another pass.

---

## Editing tricks that work fast

- **Add one real anecdote** per section — instant humanity
- **Vary paragraph length** — single-line paragraphs are allowed
- **Kill hedge soup** — "it's important to note that" factories
- **Swap generic examples** for ones you've actually used
- **Check facts** — models confide nonsense confidently

---

## When I don't bother humanizing

Internal summaries, first-pass research dumps, outline expansion where I'll rewrite anyway — **good enough is good enough.**

When it ships under my name on JoRap.com, the bar is higher. That's what editing is for.

## Bottom line

Don't ask AI to sound human in the abstract. Ask for **burstiness and perplexity**, then add the parts only you have — experience, taste, and facts.

The goal isn't to fool readers. It's to **start further down the page** so your editing time goes to substance, not untangling robot rhythm.
