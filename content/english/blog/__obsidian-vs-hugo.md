**Private notes vs Hugo** isn't winner-take-all. It's two rooms in the same house.

I used to keep a separate wiki-style app for private thinking and Hugo for the public site. I don't do that anymore. **JoRap Notes** is the public garden on this Hugo build. Private stuff stays in [the garage](/notes/the-garage-concept/) - drafts, offline captures, ideas not ready to publish - not a parallel vault I have to sync.

---

## Private richness, public polish

Explode wikilinks in the garden; **clean URLs and nav on site.** Copy or distill when something is ready to ship.

The public side gets taxonomy, theme, search, and a CDN deploy. The private side gets mess, half-sentences, and permission to be wrong.

---

## Git as bridge

Some folks keep drafts in repo `content/drafts/` or unpublished frontmatter. **Same files, different build targets.**

My version: garden notes are Markdown in Git; what you see at `/notes/` is the subset I'm willing to grow in public.

---

## Don't duplicate maintenance

Pick one capture habit and one publish pipeline. **One direction** - garage → garden → push, not two masters fighting over the same idea.

When I maintained a separate note app and a Hugo site, I spent weekends syncing instead of writing. Cutting the extra app was the lazy fix.


Private vs public is workflow design. **Think private, ship public.** Markdown travels; you don't need a second wiki host to make that true.
