# Duopoly

**Players:** 2  
**Time:** ~15 minutes

Two firms compete for control of seven countries. Deal cards are influence in each market. Whoever holds more influence in a country controls it and earns its point value. Most actions are **I split, you choose** - you package offers, your rival picks what hurts you least.

---

## Contents

- **7 country cards** (double-sided): Germany (2), France (2), UK (2), China (3), Japan (3), EU (4), US (5)
- **21 deal cards** (3 per country)
- **8 action cards** (4 per player): Secret, Trade-off, Gift, Competition

### Country cards

**Neutral (no controller):** Two identical country flags. Country name and points in **black** on both halves.

**Controlled:** One flag. Country name and points in **green** facing the controlling player and **red** facing the rival. Orient green toward whoever leads that market after scoring.

### Action cards

Each player keeps 4 action cards face up. When you use one, flip it face down (once per round).

| Action card | Deal cards |
|-------------|------------|
| **Secret** | 1 card face-down **under** the card. Peek allowed. Reveal at scoring. |
| **Trade-off** | 2 cards face-down **under** the card. Out of play this round. |
| **Gift** | 3 cards face-up on the table. Rival picks 1; you keep 2. |
| **Competition** | 4 cards face-up as two pairs. Rival picks one pair; you keep the other. |

Gift and Competition cards go next to the matching country on the table. Only Secret and Trade-off tuck cards under the action card.

---

## Objective

Win immediately at the end of a scoring phase if you control either:

- **4 or more countries**, or
- **11 or more market-value points**

Each country prints its value on the card and on matching deal cards:

| Country | Points |
|---------|--------|
| US | 5 |
| EU | 4 |
| China | 3 |
| Japan | 3 |
| Germany | 2 |
| France | 2 |
| UK | 2 |

21 points total across all seven markets. If both players hit a win condition in the same round, **points beat country count**.

---

## Preparation

1. Lay out the **7 country cards** in a row between players, left to right: **Germany, France, UK, China, Japan, EU, US** (low to high by point value). **Neutral side up** (double flag, black text).
2. Shuffle the **21 deal cards**.
3. Each player takes their **4 action cards** and places them face up.
4. Remove **1 random deal card** unseen. It stays out for the round.
5. Deal **6 cards** to each player. The rest form a draw pile.
6. Younger player goes first (or flip a coin).

---

## How to Play

Each round: **Deal → Action → Scoring**. If nobody wins, start a new round. Country cards stay flipped and oriented between rounds.

### Action phase (8 turns)

Players alternate. On your turn:

1. Draw **1** deal card.
2. Use **1** face-up action card (any order).
3. Resolve the action (tuck under for Secret and Trade-off; play face-up for Gift and Competition).
4. Flip that action card face down.

After both players have taken four turns, go to Scoring.

### The four actions

| Action card | What you do |
|-------------|-------------|
| **Secret** | Tuck 1 deal card face-down under the Secret card. |
| **Trade-off** | Tuck 2 deal cards face-down under the Trade-off card. |
| **Gift** | Reveal 3 cards. Rival picks 1 for their side; you keep 2 on yours. |
| **Competition** | Reveal 4 cards as two pairs of 2. Rival picks one pair; you keep the other. |

### Between rounds

- Pick up deal cards from the country row. Reshuffle, remove 1 card, deal 6 each. Trade-off cards stay out of the deck.
- Country cards do **not** flip back to neutral.
- Flip all 4 action cards face up.
- Second player from the last round goes first.

---

## Scoring

1. Lift the deal card from under each **Secret** action card. Reveal and place on the country row.
2. For each country, count deal cards on each side.
   - **More on your side** → flip to the single-flag side, **green toward you, red toward your rival**.
   - **Tie, or no cards played** → card stays as-is (neutral stays neutral; controlled markets keep current orientation).
3. Count countries you control (green facing you) and their point values.
4. If either player has **4+ countries** or **11+ points**, that player wins. Otherwise, start a new round.

**Example:** Germany (2) starts neutral. You have two Germany deal cards on your side, your rival has one. Flip the Germany card green toward you. Next round you tie 2-2 there - the card does not reorient. You still control Germany.
