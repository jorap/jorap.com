AI-generated articles often fail the vibe check before the fact check. Not because the model is useless - because the output is **too smooth, too even, too same-length every sentence.**

Humans don't write like that. We burst. We shorten. We take a weird detour and come back.

---

## Perplexity and burstiness (plain English)

**Perplexity** - how varied and surprising the word choices feel. Flat mid perplexity reads robotic.

**Burstiness** - variation in sentence length. Models default to same-shaped middle paragraphs. Humans don't.

When I want a draft that survives a human read, I ask for **both** by name in the prompt.

---

## The prompt I paste now

You'll see a viral version everywhere that starts with "When it comes to writing content, two factors are crucial…" I kept that in my old notes as a warning label, not a template. Don't copy it.

What I paste:

```
Write with high perplexity and burstiness - mix short sentences with longer ones, vary paragraph length, and don't make every sentence the same shape.

Topic and notes:
```

Then I paste the brief, outline, or rough notes - never "write me an article about X" with nothing attached.

---

## Before and after (real edit)

**AI draft:**

> Wireless earphones have become an essential accessory for modern users. Comfort, battery life, and sound quality all get equal bullet points. Many consumers prioritize noise cancellation, while others prefer open-ear designs for situational awareness.

**After one pass:**

> I've been through a lot of wireless earphones. Most of them ended up in a drawer within a month. What I care about now is whether I'll still be wearing them at the end of the day - and whether I can hear the jeepney behind me on a walk.

Same topic. One got specifics, opinion, and a scene. The other could be any tech blog.

---

## What still needs a human pass

AI can mimic shape. It can't replace **your** stories, opinions, local context, or judgment about what's true today.

My workflow: **AI draft → add one real detail per section → cut 20% → read aloud.**

If it sounds like a brochure when spoken, it goes back for another pass.

Quick fixes that work:

- One real anecdote per section
- Single-line paragraphs allowed
- Cut hedge soup - the "important to note that…" factories
- Swap generic examples for ones you've actually lived
- Check facts - models confide nonsense confidently

---

## When I don't bother

Internal summaries, research dumps, outlines I'll rewrite anyway - **good enough is good enough.**

When it ships under my name on jorap.com, the bar is higher. That's what editing is for.

---

Don't ask AI to sound human in the abstract. Ask for **burstiness and perplexity**, then add the parts only you have - experience, taste, and facts.

The goal isn't to fool readers. It's to **start further down the page** so your editing time goes to substance, not untangling robot rhythm.
